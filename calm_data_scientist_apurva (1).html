How I’d use data at Calm

1 · Start from the business question
  • Work with product and growth partners to clarify the question: e.g., “How do we improve D30 retention for new sleep-focused users?”
  • Translate this into a clear metric framework (e.g., D1 / D7 / D30 retention, feature adoption, funnel conversion) and a list of cuts we care about (platform, geography, channel).
2 · Explore, segment, and understand behavior
  • Use SQL + Python/R to dig into engagement data: which sessions people use, how often they return, and what sequences correlate with long-term retention.
  • Build views and dashboards in tools like Amplitude / Tableau / Mode so stakeholders can self-serve insights and monitor the metrics we define together.
3 · Design experiments & measure impact
  • Partner with product teams to design A/B tests—for example, new onboarding flows or personalized recommendations—and define the success metrics and guardrails.
  • Analyze experiment results, accounting for sample sizes, power, and multiple comparisons where appropriate, and clearly recommend whether to ship, iterate, or stop.
4 · Turn insights into stories and roadmaps
  • Summarize findings with visuals + narrative: what we learned, why it matters, and what we should do next.
  • Help Calm teams prioritize product work based on impact and confidence, and keep a feedback loop to see if the changes have the expected long-term effect.
